## 13.1 Functions of Management

|--------|----------|-------------------|

| **Co** | Coordinating | Synchronizing activities |
| **R** | Reporting | Progress reports, MIS |
| **B** | Budgeting | Financial planning, allocation |

---

## 13.2 Organizational Models of ODL

### 1. Single-Mode Institutions

- **Only** offer distance education
- Dedicated resources for ODL
- **Examples**: IGNOU, OU UK, BRAOU

### 2. Dual-Mode Institutions

- Offer **both** conventional and distance programs
- Shared resources
- **Examples**: Delhi University DDE, State University DDEs

### 3. Consortium Model

- **Multiple institutions** collaborate
- Shared course development
- **Examples**: UGC-CEC, SWAYAM

# Unit 14: Quality Assurance in ODE

## 14.1 What is Quality in ODL?

**Quality** = Fitness for purpose + Meeting stakeholder expectations

### Dimensions of Quality (Garvin)

1. **Performance** - Learning outcomes
2. **Features** - Additional services
3. **Reliability** - Consistency
4. **Conformance** - Meeting standards
5. **Durability** - Long-term relevance
6. **Serviceability** - Support services
7. **Aesthetics** - Presentation
8. **Perceived Quality** - Reputation

# Unit 15: Economics of ODE

## 15.1 Education as Investment

### Human Capital Theory

- **Education** = Investment in human capital
- Returns: Higher income, better employment
- Individual + Social benefits

### Education as Consumption vs Investment

| Consumption View | Investment View |
|-----------------|-----------------|
| Present satisfaction | Future returns |
| Personal benefit | Economic growth |
| Cultural enrichment | Skill development |

